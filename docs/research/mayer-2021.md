# Mayer (2021) — Social Cues, Personalization, and Learning Retention

**Citation (APA):**
Mayer, R. E. (2021). Principles of multimedia learning based on social cues: Personalization, voice, and image principles. In R. E. Mayer & L. Fiorella (Eds.), *The Cambridge handbook of multimedia learning* (3rd ed., pp. 268–280). Cambridge University Press.

---

## Summary

- The **Personalization Principle**: presenting instructional content in conversational rather than formal style deepens learning — supported across 10 of 10 experiments with a median effect size of 1.3.
- The **Voice Principle**: a standard-accented human voice outperforms machine voices and foreign-accented voices for learning retention (median *d* = 0.8 across 4 comparisons).
- The **Embodiment Principle**: on-screen agents that display eye contact, gestures, gaze direction, and facial expressions produce better learning outcomes than static or expressionless agents.
- Mayer's Social Agency Theory explains this: social cues prime social responses in learners, leading to deeper cognitive processing and better test performance — not mere preference.
- Together, these principles establish that *who* delivers instruction (persona characteristics, voice, relational style) directly affects how deeply content is processed and retained.

**Key Quote:**
> "The personalization principle...was supported in 10 out of 10 experimental tests, yielding a median effect size of 1.3."
