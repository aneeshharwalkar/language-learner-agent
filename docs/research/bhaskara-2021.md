## Summary

- Examined automation transparency as an independent system variable across three levels in a simulated unmanned vehicle management task with an unreliable automated recommender.
- **Reasoning transparency** (exposing the logic behind automated recommendations) significantly improved operator accuracy in deciding when to follow vs. override the system — *without* increasing workload or decision time.
- **Outcome visualization transparency** (displaying projected decision outcomes visually) paradoxically *reduced* accuracy and induced automation bias — operators over-relied on the system regardless of its quality.
- Key design insight: transparency that explains *why* a system made a recommendation supports appropriate reliance; transparency that simply shows *what* a system predicts can suppress critical evaluation.
- Directly applicable to language learning tools: Duolingo's progress bars, crowns, and skill percentages display outcome signals (what you've earned) without explaining the gap between measured skills and conversational competence, amplifying misplaced learner confidence.

**Key Quote:**
> "Providing the reasoning underlying automation improved accuracy of automaton use" while "providing visualizations of projected outcomes impaired accuracy of automaton use."
