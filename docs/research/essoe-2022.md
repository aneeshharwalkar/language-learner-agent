# Essoe et al. (2022) — VR Context Reinstatement & Language Retention

**Citation (APA):**
Essoe, J. K.-Y., Reggente, N., Ohno, A. A., Baek, Y. H., Dell'Italia, J., & Rissman, J. (2022). Enhancing learning and retention with distinctive virtual reality environments and mental context reinstatement. *npj Science of Learning*, *7*, 31. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41539-022-00147-6

---

## Summary

- Participants learned 80 foreign vocabulary items across two phonetically similar languages; those in separate virtual environments retained **92%** at one-week follow-up versus **76%** in the same-context condition.
- The benefit was mediated by *mental context reinstatement* — actively re-imagining the original learning environment during recall triggered measurable memory retrieval advantages.
- An fMRI follow-up confirmed that higher representational fidelity between encoding and recall brain activity was associated with a ~5% recall improvement.
- Separate-context learners produced **38% fewer cross-language intrusions**, demonstrating reduced interference — directly relevant to dialect-specific learning.
- Critical moderator: the retention benefit required participants to perceive the VR environment as a genuine space (high "presence"); passive screen-watching did not produce the effect.

**Key Quote / Paraphrase:**
> *[Paraphrase of reported results — verify against abstract before using as a direct quotation in slides]:* Participants who learned each language in its own unique VR context showed improved one-week retention (~92%) compared to those who learned both languages in the same context (~76%).

**Rubric Phase:** Phase 4 — provides neural and behavioral evidence that distinct learning contexts improve retention, directly motivating the use of persona/dialect-specific conversation agents.
