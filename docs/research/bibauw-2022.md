# Bibauw et al. (2022) — Meta-Analysis of Dialogue-Based CALL

**Citation (APA):**
Bibauw, S., Van den Noortgate, W., François, T., & Desmet, P. (2022). Dialogue systems for language learning: A meta-analysis. *Language Learning & Technology*, *26*(1), 1–24. http://hdl.handle.net/10125/73488

---

## Summary

- Three-level meta-analysis of 17 effectiveness studies on **dialogue-based CALL** — systems in which learners practice an L2 by interacting with a conversational agent (chatbot, dialogue system, or spoken-dialogue tutor).
- Aggregated across k = 100 individual effect sizes via a multilevel random-effects model; overall effect on L2 proficiency was **d = 0.58** (medium), statistically significant.
- Moderator analyses found stronger effects for **form-focused, goal-oriented systems** that provide **corrective feedback** and **gamification features**, with the largest gains for lower-proficiency learners and on vocabulary, morphosyntax, and holistic proficiency.
- System-guided interaction outperformed fully free conversation — a design implication directly relevant to this project's dialect-specific agent: the agent should scaffold the interaction, not merely respond.
- Supplies the **intervention-side evidence base** for this project's own proposed solution: conversational-agent practice does produce measurable L2 gains, with effect sizes at roughly two-thirds the magnitude of the immediate-post-test MALL effect reported by Mihaylova et al. (2022), but on a conversational outcome the MALL studies did not measure.

**Key Quote:**
> "Dialogue-based CALL practice had a significant medium effect size on L2 proficiency development (d = 0.58)."
