# Godwin-Jones (2018) — Contextualized Vocabulary Learning

**Citation (APA):**
Godwin-Jones, R. (2018). Contextualized vocabulary learning. *Language Learning & Technology*, *22*(3), 1–19.

---

## Summary

- Most popular vocabulary apps (including Duolingo) present words in isolated, decontextualized units — prioritizing memorization efficiency over situational meaning and pragmatic use.
- Research cited within argues that vocabulary learned in rich social and situational contexts transfers more readily to real-world production than vocabulary drilled in isolation.
- Spaced repetition systems (SRS), despite strong memorization outcomes, do not build the pragmatic knowledge needed to deploy words appropriately in conversation.
- Godwin-Jones calls for task-based, input-rich, and socially embedded vocabulary instruction as the path toward communicative transfer.
- Highlights the gap between *knowing* a word and *using* it — a distinction critical to conversational fluency that apps systematically underaddress.

**Key Quote:**
> Apps "teach vocabulary in isolated units rather than relevant contexts... despite a pedagogical shift toward communicative approaches, these apps are behaviorist in nature."

---

**Rubric Phase:** Phase 2 — articulates the pedagogical mechanism behind transfer failure; directly supports the argument that decontextualized drill cannot produce conversational competence.
