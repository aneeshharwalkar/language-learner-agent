# Jiang et al. (2021) — Duolingo Reading & Listening Outcomes

**Citation (APA):**
Jiang, X., Rollinson, J., Plonsky, L., Gustafson, E., & Pajak, B. (2021). Evaluating the reading and listening outcomes of beginning-level Duolingo courses. *Foreign Language Annals*, *54*(4), 974–1002. https://doi.org/10.1111/flan.12600

---

## Summary

- 225 adult learners who used Duolingo (Spanish or French) as their *only* learning tool were assessed using ACTFL proficiency rubrics.
- Learners reached **Intermediate Low in reading** and **Novice High in listening**, roughly equivalent to four semesters of university-level study.
- The study design explicitly excluded assessment of speaking and writing: "No other skills were assessed."
- This omission reveals a structural blind spot — Duolingo's own evidence base covers only receptive skills, leaving productive/conversational ability unvalidated.
- The proficiency ceiling (Intermediate Low / Novice High) highlights a gap between app-mediated learning and real conversational fluency, which typically begins at Intermediate Mid–High.

**Key Quote:**
> "No other skills were assessed."
