# Kristensen et al. (2024) — Repeated Mistakes in App-Based Language Learning

**Citation (APA):**
Kristensen, J. K., Torkildsen, J. v. K., & Andersson, B. (2024). Repeated mistakes in app-based language learning: Persistence and relation to learning gains. *Computers & Education*, *210*, 104966. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compedu.2023.104966

---

## Summary

- Tracked 363 Norwegian second-graders using a vocabulary app over 8 weeks; measured error repetition patterns and final learning gains.
- Error repetition rates remained **stable over time** — this behavior did not diminish naturally with continued practice, indicating apps alone do not self-correct the problem.
- Students who repeated mistakes more frequently showed significantly **lower learning gains** across all levels of prior knowledge.
- Authors describe a "double threat": error repetition both reduces exposure to correct forms *and* increases exposure to incorrect ones, compounding the transfer deficit.
- Recommends automated detection of error-repetition patterns and explanatory corrective feedback, neither of which most gamified apps currently provide.

**Key Quote:**
> "The propensity to repeat mistakes remained stable over time."

---

**Rubric Phase:** Phase 2 — documents a concrete mechanism of transfer failure in app-based learning and motivates the need for adaptive, context-aware feedback.
