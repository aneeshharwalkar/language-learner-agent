# Cannon-Bowers & Salas (1998) — TADMUS: Making Decisions Under Stress

**Citation (APA):**
Cannon-Bowers, J. A., & Salas, E. (Eds.). (1998). *Making decisions under stress: Implications for individual and team training*. American Psychological Association. (ISBN: 978-1-55798-767-9, 447 pp.) https://doi.org/10.1037/10278-000

---

## Summary

- Edited volume reporting the culmination of the seven-year **TADMUS** (Tactical Decision Making Under Stress) research program, sponsored by the Office of Naval Research following the 1988 USS Vincennes incident.
- Establishes the empirical basis for the HFACS analysis's repeated invocation of "TADMUS principles" — closes QA WARNING #5, which flagged TADMUS as uncited background knowledge.
- Core TADMUS finding relevant to this project: operator performance under cognitive load depends on **timely, accurate, diagnostic feedback that updates the operator's mental model of the task environment**; feedback that is merely encouraging or that signals the wrong variable degrades rather than supports decision quality.
- Volume is organized around individual-level training (Part II) and team-level training (Part III), with Part IV on decision support and display principles — the direct ancestor of modern automation-transparency research (Bhaskara et al., 2021) also cited in this project.
- Applied to Duolingo: the supervisory algorithm provides engagement-positive feedback (streaks, XP, crown levels) that is neither diagnostic of conversational competence nor actionable for the learner's mental model — a textbook violation of the TADMUS feedback principle.
