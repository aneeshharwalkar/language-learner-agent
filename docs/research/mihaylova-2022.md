# Mihaylova et al. (2022) — Meta-Analysis on Mobile-Assisted Language Learning

**Citation (APA):**
Mihaylova, M., Gorin, S., Reber, T. P., & Rothen, N. (2022). A meta-analysis on mobile-assisted language learning applications: Benefits and risks. *Psychologica Belgica*, *62*(1), 252–271. https://doi.org/10.5334/pb.1146

---

## Summary

- Synthesized 23 studies and found a moderate-to-strong overall effect favoring mobile language apps over traditional classroom instruction (*g* = 0.88) on immediate post-tests.
- However, **only 3 of 23 studies** included delayed follow-up assessments; those that did showed only small effect sizes at delayed post-tests, suggesting short-lived gains.
- 78% of included studies had high risk of bias; only 22% used randomization, making causal claims about transfer unreliable.
- Most studies examined short intervention windows, leaving long-term transfer and real-world communicative competence unaddressed.
- GRADE assessment rated overall evidence quality as "low" to "very low," cautioning against strong conclusions about app-based learning efficacy.

**Key Quote:**
> "Findings should be treated with caution due to risks of high bias."

---

**Rubric Phase:** Phase 2 — establishes that the evidence base for app-based language learning transfer is weaker than popular perception suggests, motivating new design approaches.
