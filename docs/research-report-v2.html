Research Report — Language Learner Agent

Deploying conversational agents to bridge the learning-transfer gap in Duolingo
Applied Human Factors · Spring 2026 Evaluate → Analyze → Improve Preston Cusick + Aneesh Harwalkar Due April 24, 2026
v2 · Simplified
01 Thesis

Duolingo produces measurable receptive-skill gains but not conversational competence. The gap is a system failure, not user failure — holes open at every HFACS level, and the Swiss Cheese lines up.

This v2 dashboard maps 14 sources to the five-phase rubric, walks the HFACS chain, names the enemy, and proposes a redesign grounded in HF principles.

02 Five-Phase Rubric Map
Phase 1 · System & Context 20 pts
Duolingo as a human-machine system under internal, external, and organizational stressors
Internal stressors: cognitive load, metacognitive illusion of fluency. External: streak pressure, XP gamification. Organizational: DAU incentives (Innovator's Dilemma). The ceiling is real — and invisible from inside the app.
Jiang (2021) · Bicknell & Brust (2020) · Kornell & Bjork (2008)
Phase 2 · Task & User Analysis 10 pts
Cognitive, perceptual, and motor demands on the learner
The four HF demand lenses: Attention narrowed to XP/streaks; Memory using only the Phonological Loop, not the Episodic Buffer; Workload over-loaded on gamification, under-loaded on production; Cognition never builds pragmatic or sociolinguistic knowledge.
Godwin-Jones (2018) · Kristensen (2024) · Mihaylova (2022) · Geeslin & Long (2014)
Phase 3 · Data & Methodology 10 pts
Research-and-build, with a proposed future RCT
No primary data given the two-week window — a known threat to validity. We use two meta-analyses as the evidence base and propose an RCT modeled on Wang's 12-week structure (extended in duration), where Agent Type is the IV and speaking performance the DV.
Mihaylova (2022) · Bibauw (2022) · Wang (2024)
Phase 4 · HF Analysis & Findings 20 pts
HFACS walk across all four levels — see next section
Performance breakdowns identified at every layer of Reason's Swiss Cheese. Each level gets a named failure and cited mechanism. Team Transparency appears here as an IV (a manipulated variable in our redesign), not a constant.
Baddeley (2000) · Cannon-Bowers & Salas (1998, TADMUS) · Bhaskara (2021) · Essoe (2022) · Mayer (2021)
Phase 5 · Redesign & Recommendations 20 pts
Dialect-specific conversational agents — feasible and actionable
A proof-of-concept that scaffolds interaction, uses an embodied social persona, and surfaces sociolinguistic variation. Solutions tied to findings; system map illustrates the redesigned information flow; gap analysis exposes remaining blind spots.
Bibauw (2022) · Wang (2024) · Mayer (2021) · Geeslin & Long (2014)
03 HFACS — Four Levels, Holes Aligned

Work backward through all four HFACS levels. Avoid blaming individuals — look for system breakdowns.

L1 · Unsafe Acts
Skill-based errors with no signal
Learners practice routines that produce wrong outputs. XP and streaks are confident feedback about the wrong variable — a False Alarm on conversational competence.
Jiang (2021) · Kristensen (2024) · Kornell & Bjork (2008)
L2 · Preconditions
Episodic Buffer foreclosed by design
Working Memory needs context-bound encoding; Duolingo engages only the Phonological Loop. Distinct contexts produce ~92% retention vs. ~76% in uniform contexts.
Baddeley (2000) · Essoe (2022) · Godwin-Jones (2018)
L3 · Unsafe Supervision
The algorithm optimizes the wrong variable
Birdbrain matches item difficulty to learner ability — an item-correctness optimizer. It never detects, adapts to, or gives feedback on conversational performance.
Bicknell & Brust (2020) · Cannon-Bowers & Salas (1998)
L4 · Organizational Influences
DAU incentives select against transfer
The Innovator's Dilemma in motion: engagement KPIs reward the features that forecast poorly for transfer. Confident progress indicators amplify a mis-calibrated learner model.
Mayer (2021) · Bhaskara (2021) · Jiang (2021)
Reading the chain: organizational KPIs exclude speaking (L4) → the algorithm never measures speaking (L3) → the environment provides no contextual encoding (L2) → the learner has no signal to act on (L1). The incident — failure to transfer — is structurally predicted.
04 Name the Enemy
1
The Decontextualized Drill
Vocabulary taught as isolated items, not in socially embedded context. Memorizes; doesn't build pragmatic knowledge.
2
The Wrong Signal
Streaks and XP are transparent about the wrong variable. Confident displays of a bad metric suppress corrective behavior.
3
The Receptive-Only Evidence Base
Duolingo's own outcome research measures reading and listening. Speaking is unsupervised by design.
4
The Aligned Holes
The Swiss Cheese stack — L4 KPIs, L3 algorithm, L2 environment, L1 learner — all open in the same place.
05 Intervention — Three Pillars, Each Sourced
Dialogue works
Bibauw (2022), meta-analysis of 17 studies: dialogue-based CALL produces a medium effect (d = 0.58) on L2 proficiency. Stronger when the system scaffolds.
Persona matters
Mayer (2021): personalization d = 1.3 across 10/10 experiments. Conversational style + embodiment invokes Social Agency Theory.
Dialect is the input
Geeslin & Long (2014): communicative competence requires acquiring sociolinguistic variation, not a standardized idealization.
Honest note (don't be afraid to report findings that go against the grain). Wang (2024) — 12-week quasi-experiment, n = 99 — found GenAI chatbots improved willingness to communicate and reduced anxiety, but did not move measured speaking performance. A proof-of-concept is proof of plausibility, not proof of effect.

07 Gap Analysis

A gap analysis can expose blind spots and missing system components. Here are the honest holes.

GAP
No primary data of our own
Two-week timeline — can't make causal claims about the proof-of-concept. Phase 5 proposes a future RCT.
GAP
No direct evidence that dialect-specific > generic agent
Geeslin & Long supports the theory; no A/B evaluation exists. Candidate for the future-study proposal.
GAP
Wang (2024) measures WTC and anxiety, not speaking performance
Affective gains; no measurable speaking lift in 12 weeks. Stated plainly in the intervention section.

Eye-tracking (IX Lab) could sharpen the L1 attention claim
